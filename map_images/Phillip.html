Activities of Philip the Evangelizer

The Bible records some of the zealous activity of "Philip the evangelizer." (Acts 21:8) He was one of the "seven reputable men" who distributed food among the Greek-speaking and Hebrew-speaking disciples in Jerusalem. (Acts 6:1-6) After the death of Stephen when "all except the apostles were scattered," Philip went to Samaria; there he preached the good news and performed miracles. (Acts 8:1,4-7) Later, Jehovah's angel sent Philip to a desert road that ran from Jerusalem to Gaza. (Acts 8:26) Philip encountered an Ethiopian eunuch on that road and declared the good news to him. (Acts 8:27-38) Led away by Jehovah's spirit (Acts 8:39), Philip continued preaching, traveling from Ashdod and through other cities near the coast until he reached Caesarea. (Acts 8:40) Years later, Luke and Paul stayed at Philip's home in Caesarea. At that time, Philip "had four unmarried daughters who prophesied." - Acts 21:8,9.

  1. Jerusalem: Performs administrative work. - Acts 6:5
  2. Samaria: Preaches the good news. - Acts 8:5
  3. Desert road to Gaza: Explains the Scriptures to an Ethiopian eunuch and baptizes him. - Acts 8:26-39
  4. Coastal region: Declares the good news to all the cities. - Acts 8:40
  5. Caesarea: Philip welcomes Paul to his house. - Acts 21:8,9
